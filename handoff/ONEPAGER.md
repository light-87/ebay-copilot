# eBay Copilot — what you can now do

An AI assistant wired directly into your eBay UK account. You talk to it in plain English inside
Claude Code; it does the work against the real eBay APIs. No dashboards to learn, no copy-paste.

---

## Three things it does

### 1. List a product from just a link
Paste a product URL (e.g. an adidas page). The assistant:
- reads the title, description, price, and **every colour/size variation**,
- pulls the images and **removes the backgrounds** automatically,
- builds a single **multi-variation listing** and publishes it to eBay UK — with all the required
  item specifics filled in so it doesn't get rejected.

*What used to be 20–30 minutes of manual listing per product becomes one sentence.*

### 2. Answer questions about your store
Just ask:
- *"What are my top selling items this month?"*
- *"How's my conversion trending?"*
- *"Am I still Top Rated?"*

It pulls live numbers from eBay and answers — including flagging problems before they cost you
(e.g. a seller-rating metric drifting toward "Below Standard").

### 3. Generate a visual dashboard — yours to shape
One request produces a clean charts page — sales trend, top listings, seller standing — that you
can open, screenshot, or share. It's **fully customizable**: ask for whatever you want on it
("add a profit column", "show returns by category", "compare this month vs last") and it's added.
Once it's set up how you like it, you just say **"refresh the analytics"** in the chat — it pulls
the latest numbers from eBay and rebuilds the page. No spreadsheets, no logins, no fiddling.

## What's included in this handoff
- The assistant, installed and connected to your eBay account (works on your MacBook).
- One-command setup — clone, drop in your private credentials file, done.
- The link-to-listing engine (background removal + auto-published variations).
- The analytics dashboard generator.
- Full documentation and a rules file so it keeps working reliably.

## A live sample
A working analytics dashboard built from your **real last-30-days data** (169 sales, Top Rated,
top-10 listings driving 75% of sales) — ask for the shared link.

## Good to know
- Everything runs on **your** machine against **your** account; your credentials never leave it.
- It always asks before anything public or irreversible (publishing, ending, refunding).
- Brand-name listings (adidas etc.) carry eBay's usual brand-protection (VeRO) risk — that stays
  your call, and the assistant will remind you.
